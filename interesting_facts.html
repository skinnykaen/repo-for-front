CENTER OF EUROPE

                    Belarus is located in Eastern Europe and, due to its position, is a buffer between Western Europe and Russia. According to some reports,
                    it is in our country that the geographical center of Europe is located - 50 kilometers from Polotsk (Lake Sho).
                

                    
NOT THE SMALLEST COUNTRY

                    The area of ​​Belarus is 207 thousand square kilometers. Belgium, Switzerland and Greece could fit entirely on its territory.
                    By the way, Belarus is the largest country in Europe that has no access to the sea.
                

                    
"LIGHT EUROPE"

                    Belarus is one of the ten most wooded countries in Europe - forests occupy 38.8% of its territory. 
                    But at the same time, our country is one of the few countries in Europe where 
                    there is no sea or real mountains. The highest point in Belarus - Dzerzhinskaya Gora - has a height of only 345 meters, and 
                    the largest reservoir is Lake Naroch with an area of ​​79.62 square kilometers.
                    Belarus is also called "the lungs of Europe" - about 30% of the country's territory is occupied by swampy lands.
                

                    
IN BELARUS, THE MOST OF THE POPULATION SPEAKS RUSSIAN

                    The most common language in Belarus is Russian. About 80% of the population speaks it at home. However, the names of settlements are 
                    often written only in Belarusian, as are the names of streets. Announcements in public transport are also heard in Belarusian.
                    About 4% of the population of Belarus speaks English.
                

                    
BELARUS HIGHLY SUFFERED FROM CHERNOBYL

                    About a quarter of the territory was polluted, the most fertile lands were damaged, and two million inhabitants had to be relocated. 
                    Today, the Polesie radiation-ecological reserve has been created in the contaminated areas.
                

                    
HOMELAND OF CELEBRITIES

                    Belarus became the homeland of the ancestors of many famous people - from Hollywood actresses Lisa Kudrow and 
                    Scarlett Johansson to Nobel laureates Zhores Alferov, Svetlana Aleksievich, hockey player Wayne Gretzky, artist 
                    Marc Chagall and many others.
                

                    
BELARUS IS IT PARADISE

                    Belarus is a country of startups. Did you know that World of Tanks games, Viber mobile messenger, 
                    Msqrd application and many others were created here? The Belarusian Hi-Tech Park is sometimes called 
                    the "Silicon Valley of Eastern Europe".
                

                    
HOUSE FOR RARE ANIMALS

                    In Belarus, there is the Belovezhskaya Pushcha National Park, the largest and oldest forest in Europe, 
                    where the largest animal in Europe, the bison, lives.
